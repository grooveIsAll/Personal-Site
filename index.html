Robert Velasco

Since childhood I have loved language, art and music. Whether writing a story, sketching in my notebook or crafting a funky groove, the act of creating from thought to final product is as addicting as is rewarding.

Inspiration to become a Software Engineer came from twelve-year-old boy giving a TEDTalk describing how he taught himself to code his own IOS app. From there I picked up a book on app development and began building small applications using XCode and Objective-C. A friend of mine would later turn me on to Codecademy, and I would go on to partake in Codecademy Labs, a twelve week Web Development Program. After completing the course I knew I wanted to go all in and make a major career change, so last Sepember I packed up and moved to San Francisco to attend MakerSquare an immersive Software Engineering Program. There I learned all the skills necessary to jump into a role as a Full-Stack Engineer!

Fetch

Fetch is a web application that connects local animal shelters to dog lovers in their area. Shelters can add dogs directly to our database and control which dogs are available to be "fetched". Users can then select a dog based on their desired activity. Using an algorithm based on breed, availability, and number of times fetched, a dog is selected and delivered to the user.
